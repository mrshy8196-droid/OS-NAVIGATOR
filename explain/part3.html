Deadlock Management Analysis

1. Understanding Deadlock Dynamics (Pages 37-41)

In Operating Systems, a Deadlock occurs when a group of processes is permanently blocked. According to pages 37-41, four conditions must coexist:

  • Mutual Exclusion: Resources are non-sharable.
  • Hold and Wait: Process holds resources while waiting for others.
  • No Preemption: Resources cannot be taken forcibly.
  • Circular Wait: A circular chain of processes waiting for each other.

2. Strategic Prevention & Avoidance (Pages 42-45)

Deadlock Prevention: Aims to design the system to exclude at least one of the four conditions.

Deadlock Avoidance: The OS performs a 'Safety Check' before allocation to ensure the system stays in a Safe State.

3. Banker's Algorithm & Recovery (Pages 46-48)

The Banker's Algorithm is the primary method for avoidance in multi-resource systems. If deadlock occurs, recovery involves:

  • Process Termination: Killing processes to break the cycle.
  • Resource Preemption: Selecting a 'victim' and rolling back its state.
